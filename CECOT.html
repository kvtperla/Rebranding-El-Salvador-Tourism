Ruhi Cenet: CECOT Mega‑Prison YouTube Documentary (2025)

Short Description: This YouTube documentary by Ruhi Cenet showcases the CECOT mega‑prison, presenting it as a symbol of El Salvador’s transformation under the government’s security policies (Cenet, 2025). The video highlights the prison’s scale, strict control, and the narrative of national safety promoted by the administration.

Perhaps the most striking example of Bukele’s branding strategy is the CECOT mega‑prison documentary, as it showcases a government‑supported video of the CECOT prison and the security they claim protects Salvadorans from gang members (Cenet, 2025). The prison is presented as proof of safety, encoding El Salvador as a nation that has conquered intense violence and gang activity (Hall, 1980). International audiences often decode this as evidence of progression and prosperity, while Salvadorans may criticize this form of advertisement as propaganda masking repression in the country.

The prison becomes a mythic symbol of order and change, forming itself as a timeless feature of Salvadoran identity and future (Barthes, 1972). Yet, the advertisement continues to erase the historical struggle that Salvadorans endured prior and only displays the safety of the country today. Counterpublic narratives are silenced through advertisements like these, particularly those concerned with human rights abuses and authoritarianism (Fraser, 1990). This reflects the lived reality of many Salvadorans today who fear being accused of gang affiliation and ending up in a prison like CECOT.

The Bukele administration commodified this idea of safety for viewers, marketing El Salvador as a country of progression from its past (Marx, 1867). The country itself becomes a product not only for tourists, but for international investors because of its publicized transformation into “the safest country in the world” in only five years. Through advertisements like the CECOT Mega‑Prison documentary, it becomes clear how state repression can be packaged and sold as a consumable narrative of national rebirth.
